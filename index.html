Research Interest

Still at the early stage of my research journey, I am exploring the synergy between deep learning, reinforcement learning, and control theory. My goal is to investigate their principled integration as a pathway toward achieving safer, more reliable, more data-efficient, and ultimately more trustworthy embodied intelligent systems.

📈Data Matters: Synthetic Experience Generation for RL
Training reliable RL policies for robotics requires millions of environment interactions—prohibitively expensive for physical systems. I have investigated the research field of synthetic RL training data generation and augmentation leveraging generative models(diffusion&flow models), NeuralODEs. Currently I am conducting relevant baseline experiments on standard benchmarks.

🛡️Safety Matters: Constrained Learning for Safety-Critical Control
Ensuring safety is critical for real-world deployment of autonomous systems. I am interested in exploring safe RL algorithms(Control Barrier Functions, Control Lyapunov Functions, Obstacle Avoidance) that can provide formal safety guarantees during both training and deployment phases.

🤖Embodiment Matters: Sim2Real & Physical Deployment
Learning algorithms ultimately prove their value in the messy reality of hardware—sensor noise, actuator latency, and unmodeled physics. While my current explorations are simulation-based, I am systematically building technical foundations for robotics(especially aerial robotics like UAVs or eVTOLs).

🧠Intelligence Matters: an exploratory interest
I am intrigued by the emerging paradigm of Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models—systems that ground high-level semantic understanding in low-level physical control. Coming from control theory, I am particularly interested in the safety and dynamics constraints that pure CV/NLP backgrounds often overlook in VLA systems. I am keen to investigate how generative world models can interface with multimodal perception in groups working at the intersection of robot learning and foundation models.
