Welcome to my homepage! I am a sophomore in Automation(Innovation Class) at School of Artificial Intelligence and Automation, HUST; also an undergraduate researcher in learning, dynamics and control

I study learning, dynamics and control for physical systems where nonlinearity, hard constraints, and high failure costs rule out simple solutions. I take physical structure as the starting point, not as an obstacle to overcome, but as the native grammar any intelligent behavior must respect. My aim is to let the system's physics determine how these three elements should be composed, and to close the loop on real hardware, where simulation alone is never enough.

Current Work

Physically-Consistent Trajectory Generation for RL on continuous control benchmarks
Reinforcement learning in continuous control domains suffers from data scarcity, yet standard generative augmentation produces trajectories that violate physical constraints like dynamics and kinematics feasibility, torque limits, boundary conditions, etc.

I am developing a constrained generative framework that enforces physical consistency during trajectory synthesis. The approach embeds both learned transition dynamics and explicitly declared physical constraints directly into the diffusion sampling process. The resulting trajectories respect the system's physical limits by construction.
